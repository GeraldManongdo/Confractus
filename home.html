About Me

Who I Am and What I Actually Do

I got into web development because I wanted to build things that solve real problems — not just pages that look good in a mockup. My goal has always been simple: take an idea, figure out what it needs to actually work, and then build it.

I'm currently finishing my BSIT degree at National University, but I've been building websites long before the degree started. Every project taught me something the classroom didn't. Most of what I know about handling edge cases, writing maintainable code, and keeping clients happy came from working on actual projects with real deadlines.

I work with PHP, JavaScript, Firebase, MySQL, and a handful of other tools depending on what the job calls for. I'm not married to any particular stack — I care about what works, what scales, and what your users will actually enjoy using.

If you have an idea that needs to become a functioning web app, that's exactly where I come in.
